# AI Copy Quality for AS6 AI CRM

## Goal

AI-generated Telegram and email outreach should sound like concise, natural Russian sales communication. The customer should never see internal AI diagnostics, raw tags, scoring language, or robotic phrases.

## Customer-facing copy rules

### Never expose internal metadata

Draft text, email subjects, recommendations, and timeline bodies must not include:

- `Лид проявил интерес к темам`
- `intent summary`
- `temperature`
- `score`
- `qualification`
- `AI detected`

Internal values may remain in structured metadata for product logic and dashboards, but they must not be rendered into customer-facing copy.

### Translate tags into business language

Raw tags are converted into business meaning before copy is generated:

| Raw signal | Business wording |
| --- | --- |
| `crm` | автоматизация CRM |
| `ai` | AI-автоматизация продаж |
| `follow-up` | follow-up с клиентами |
| `telegram` | коммуникации в Telegram |
| `email` | email-коммуникации |
| `integration` | связка с текущими инструментами |

Example:

- Bad: `Возвращаюсь к запросу по Лид проявил интерес к темам: crm, ai..`
- Good: `Возвращаюсь к вашему запросу по автоматизации CRM и follow-up.`

## Personalization inputs

The outreach engine builds a structured copy context before composing a draft:

- first name;
- company;
- lead source;
- inferred business need;
- detected pain points;
- recommended channel.

The engine uses this context rather than injecting raw notes or AI analysis directly into templates.

## Telegram style

Telegram drafts are intentionally short:

- 2–5 short lines;
- conversational and easy to answer;
- one clear question or next step;
- no fake urgency;
- no aggressive wording.

Good CTA examples:

- `Актуально обсудить автоматизацию follow-up?`
- `Удобно коротко обсудить на этой неделе?`
- `Удобно посмотреть на этой неделе?`

## Email style

Email drafts include:

- clear subject;
- short paragraphs;
- one CTA at the end;
- no large text blocks;
- no internal AI vocabulary.

Good CTA examples:

- `Удобно показать демо на этой неделе?`
- `Удобно показать 2–3 подходящих сценария на этой неделе?`
- `Подскажите, задача ещё актуальна?`

## Safety constraints

Generated outreach must not:

- hallucinate integrations that were not confirmed;
- promise impossible ROI or guaranteed outcomes;
- use fake urgency;
- sound pushy, spammy, or manipulative;
- claim that materials were sent until SMTP/email sending actually succeeds.

## Communication types covered

The copy quality layer covers all outreach types generated by the AI Outreach Engine:

- `first_contact`;
- `followup_24h`;
- `followup_3d`;
- `meeting_request`;
- `demo_offer`.

## Timeline readability

Timeline events should be readable by managers. Titles and bodies describe what happened in business language, for example:

- `Черновик Telegram подготовлен`;
- `Черновики сообщений подготовлены`;
- `Оценка лида обновлена`;
- `Follow-up предложен`.

Scoring metadata can remain attached as JSON for analytics, but timeline text should summarize the customer situation or manager action in human language.

## Verification checklist

For a hot lead, verify that the system still creates:

1. Telegram and email drafts in `ai_worker_queue` for each outreach type.
2. Suggested follow-up jobs.
3. Readable timeline events in `lead_timeline_events`.
4. Email send logs in `email_logs` / email message records after an approved email is sent.

Quality checks:

- generated copy is natural Russian;
- Telegram drafts are 2–5 short lines;
- emails have subject, short paragraphs, and one CTA;
- no forbidden internal phrases appear in customer-facing text;
- approval queue, followups, email sending, and Telegram drafts remain intact.
